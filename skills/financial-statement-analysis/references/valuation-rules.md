# Valuation Rules

## When To Use

Use valuation work only when the user asks for valuation context, assumption review, enterprise value support, investment memo input, transaction analysis, or scenario comparison. If the task is only financial performance understanding, do not expand into valuation.

## DCF Process

1. Build the historical base from sourced financials: revenue growth, gross margin, operating margin, tax rate, depreciation and amortization, capital expenditures, working capital, and free cash flow.
2. Define the cash-flow measure. Unlevered free cash flow is usually EBIT x (1 - tax rate) + depreciation and amortization - capital expenditures - change in net working capital.
3. Forecast drivers instead of only extending totals: market size, volume, price, churn, bookings, backlog, capacity, margin, cost inflation, operating leverage, capex intensity, and working-capital turns when relevant.
4. Estimate discount rate assumptions explicitly: risk-free rate, equity risk premium, beta, cost of debt, tax rate, debt weight, and equity weight.
5. Calculate terminal value using perpetual growth, exit multiple, or both. The terminal assumption must be economically plausible relative to long-term growth and reinvestment.
6. Bridge enterprise value to equity value: add cash and non-operating assets, subtract debt, preferred equity, leases if treated as debt, minority interest, and other claims.
7. Present valuation as a range with sensitivity tables or narrative sensitivities. Do not present a single precise value as certainty.

## Comparable Valuation

- Select peers by business model, customer type, growth, margin, geography, scale, capital intensity, and accounting basis.
- Common multiples include EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, EV/EBIT, P/E, P/B, and EV/FCF. Use only multiples that make sense for the business and profit stage.
- Adjust interpretation for growth, margin, profitability, leverage, cyclicality, liquidity, governance, customer concentration, and accounting differences.
- Use market data and share counts with a clear date. Stale market data must be marked.
- Reconcile why the subject should trade at a premium, discount, or near peers; do not average peers mechanically.

## Scenario And Sensitivity

- At minimum, state base, upside, and downside drivers when uncertainty is material.
- Sensitize the assumptions that matter most: revenue growth, terminal margin, WACC, terminal growth, exit multiple, capex, working capital, churn, and customer acquisition efficiency when applicable.
- Tie scenario changes to evidence or explicit assumptions.
- Highlight breakpoints where the conclusion changes.

## Sanity Checks

- Compare DCF output against implied revenue, EBITDA, earnings, or FCF multiples.
- Compare forecast margins and reinvestment against history, peers, and industry structure.
- Check terminal value share of enterprise value; a very high share means most value depends on long-term assumptions.
- Confirm enterprise-to-equity bridge and share count before discussing per-share values.
- Label valuation as analytical support, not a trade instruction or professional appraisal.
