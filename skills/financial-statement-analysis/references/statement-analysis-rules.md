# Statement Analysis Rules

## Source Discipline

- Keep the entity, period, fiscal calendar, currency, units, source date, and accounting basis attached to each material number.
- Distinguish audited, unaudited, preliminary, pro forma, restated, consolidated, and segment-level figures.
- Reconcile totals before analysis when the input is manually copied, OCR-derived, spreadsheet-derived, or mixed from multiple reports.
- Treat missing notes, missing statement lines, unclear sign conventions, stale data, and changed definitions as analysis limitations.

## Core Analysis Modes

- Trend analysis: compare period-over-period movement in revenue, margins, expenses, assets, liabilities, equity, operating cash flow, capital expenditures, and free cash flow.
- Common-size analysis: express income-statement lines as percentage of revenue and balance-sheet lines as percentage of total assets or invested capital when helpful.
- Variance analysis: compare actuals against budget, guidance, prior period, or forecast; separate price, volume, mix, cost, timing, and one-time drivers when evidence supports it.
- Segment analysis: separate products, geographies, channels, or customer groups when consolidated results hide different economics.
- Cash-flow quality: compare net income to operating cash flow and free cash flow; inspect working capital, capex, non-cash items, and recurring conversion.

## Ratio Families

Profitability:

- Gross margin = gross profit / revenue.
- Operating margin = operating income / revenue.
- Net margin = net income / revenue.
- ROA = net income / average assets.
- ROE = net income / average equity; explain leverage effects before treating high ROE as quality.
- ROIC = NOPAT / invested capital when NOPAT and invested capital can be defined consistently.

Liquidity:

- Current ratio = current assets / current liabilities.
- Quick ratio = cash plus marketable securities plus receivables / current liabilities.
- Cash ratio = cash plus marketable securities / current liabilities.

Leverage and coverage:

- Debt-to-equity = total debt / equity.
- Net debt = debt minus cash and cash equivalents.
- Net debt / EBITDA requires a clear EBITDA definition and period.
- Interest coverage = EBIT or EBITDA / interest expense; explain which numerator is used.
- Debt service coverage requires scheduled principal and interest data; do not estimate silently.

Efficiency and working capital:

- Asset turnover = revenue / average assets.
- Inventory turnover = COGS / average inventory.
- DSO = average receivables / revenue per day.
- DIO = average inventory / COGS per day.
- DPO = average payables / COGS per day when COGS is the best available denominator.
- Cash conversion cycle = DIO + DSO - DPO.

Cash flow:

- Free cash flow = operating cash flow - capital expenditures unless the user or source defines another version.
- FCF margin = free cash flow / revenue.
- FCF conversion = free cash flow / net income or free cash flow / EBITDA; state which denominator is used.

Growth:

- YoY growth = current period / prior comparable period - 1.
- CAGR = ending value / beginning value raised to 1 / periods - 1.
- Separate organic growth, acquisition growth, price, volume, mix, currency, and timing when source material supports it.

## Interpretation Rules

- Never infer financial health from a single ratio.
- Compare ratios against the same company history, disclosed targets, peer set, or industry norm when available.
- Explain direction and magnitude; do not overstate precision when source data is rounded.
- When industry economics matter, call out capital intensity, working-capital cycle, regulation, commodity exposure, customer concentration, and revenue model.
- For negative denominators or near-zero denominators, avoid misleading percentage ratios and explain why the metric is unstable.

## Red Flags

- Revenue growth with declining cash conversion.
- Margin improvement driven only by one-time gains, capitalization, or cuts that may impair future performance.
- Rising receivables, inventory, or contract assets materially faster than revenue.
- Debt growth without matching cash generation or maturity disclosure.
- Heavy adjustments without reconciliation to GAAP/IFRS measures.
- Guidance, budget, or forecast assumptions not tied to historical drivers.
