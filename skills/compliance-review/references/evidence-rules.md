# Evidence Rules

## Evidence Quality

Assess evidence by:

- source owner;
- creation date and covered period;
- completeness;
- authenticity or traceability;
- whether it shows design only or operating effectiveness;
- whether it is a sample, full population, screenshot, log, ticket, approval, policy, report, attestation, or user statement;
- whether it can be independently verified.

## Audit Readiness

Audit-ready evidence should show:

- what requirement it supports;
- who performed the action;
- when it happened;
- what system or process was involved;
- what result occurred;
- approval or review record when required;
- retention location;
- exception handling.

## Common Weak Evidence

- policy text with no operating proof;
- screenshots with no date, owner, or scope;
- user statements without supporting records;
- stale exports;
- partial samples with no sampling logic;
- ticket comments without final resolution;
- reports where filters or source systems are unknown.

Treat weak evidence as partial or unknown, not as proof of compliance.
