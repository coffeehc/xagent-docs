# Investment Research Rules

## Source Hierarchy

Prefer sources in this order when available:

1. Regulatory filings, annual reports, quarterly reports, audited financial statements, prospectuses, and official exchange or regulator records.
2. Company investor presentations, earnings releases, call transcripts, press releases, product documentation, pricing pages, and management commentary.
3. Reputable market data, industry data, customer data, expert material, reputable news, and recognized benchmark sources with dates.
4. Analyst summaries, newsletters, databases, blogs, forums, or unsourced summaries only as leads that require verification.

Record source date, event date, reporting period, and market-data date separately. Do not present stale data as current.

## Research Modes

- Company research: business model, products, customers, revenue drivers, margin structure, capital intensity, management, governance, and strategic position.
- Public-equity research: filings, earnings, guidance, financial quality, valuation, ownership, liquidity, catalysts, sentiment, and risk.
- Private investment research: market, team, product, traction, unit economics, competitive moat, financing terms, exit paths, dilution, and diligence gaps.
- Credit research: cash-flow durability, leverage, coverage, collateral, covenants, maturity wall, liquidity, refinancing risk, and downside recovery.
- Sector research: market size, growth drivers, regulation, value chain, profit pools, competitive forces, winners and losers, and cycle sensitivity.
- Event research: earnings, M&A, financing, regulation, product launch, litigation, supply disruption, management change, or macro shock.

## Thesis Construction

Build research around evidence, not narrative first:

- What is the core question?
- What is the asset or company exposed to?
- What facts are established?
- What assumptions are required?
- What is the variant view or differentiated insight?
- What evidence supports the view?
- What evidence weakens or contradicts the view?
- What risks can impair the case?
- What catalysts or monitoring indicators matter?
- What diligence remains before confidence can rise?

## Risk Categories

Consider only risks relevant to the asset:

- business model risk;
- customer, supplier, channel, or concentration risk;
- competitive and disruption risk;
- margin, cost, and operating leverage risk;
- liquidity, leverage, refinancing, and covenant risk;
- valuation and market multiple risk;
- macro, rates, currency, commodity, and cycle risk;
- regulation, litigation, compliance, and policy risk;
- governance, management, related-party, and incentive risk;
- execution, integration, technology, security, and product risk;
- data-quality, source-quality, and freshness risk.

## Language Boundaries

- Prefer "research view", "evidence suggests", "base case", "downside case", "monitor", "requires diligence", and "not verified".
- Avoid presenting conclusions as personalized instructions or guaranteed outcomes.
- If the user asks for a direct transaction recommendation, reframe to evidence, scenarios, risks, and questions a human decision-maker should review.
- Make uncertainty legible. A good research note shows what could change the conclusion.
