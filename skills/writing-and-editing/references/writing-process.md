# Writing Process Rules

## Edit Depth

Choose the smallest edit that satisfies the task.

- Light polish: grammar, clarity, flow, and minor wording.
- Structural edit: reorganize sections, ordering, headings, and emphasis.
- Rewrite: rebuild the text while preserving facts and intent.
- Critique: identify issues and recommend changes before rewriting.
- Communication draft: write a message with a clear recipient, context, ask, and next step.
- Tone adaptation: change style or formality without changing meaning.

## Fact Preservation

Treat the user's facts as source material.

- Preserve names, numbers, dates, decisions, constraints, and commitments.
- Do not upgrade uncertainty into certainty.
- Do not add supporting claims that were not provided.
- If a claim needs evidence, mark it as missing or ask for the source.
- Keep sensitive or internal-only facts out of external-facing copy unless the user explicitly includes them for that audience.

## Reader Fit

Before drafting, decide what the reader needs first:

- answer first: direct response or executive context;
- ask first: requests, approvals, blockers, scheduling;
- context first: sensitive updates or complex background;
- evidence first: disputed claims or high-stakes recommendations;
- action first: task instructions or operational handoff.

Use the minimum context needed for the reader to act.

## Tone Rules

Tone should fit relationship and channel.

- Direct: short sentences, active voice, clear ask.
- Polite: acknowledge context, avoid blame, keep the ask specific.
- Formal: precise wording, fewer idioms, visible structure.
- Executive: conclusion first, implications, decision needed.
- Customer-facing: approved facts only, no internal uncertainty unless intentionally disclosed.
- Apologetic: acknowledge impact, state fix or next step, avoid excuses.

## Communication Drafting

For emails, messages, replies, or announcements:

1. Identify sender, recipient, relationship, and channel.
2. Name the purpose in one line.
3. Include only necessary context.
4. Make the ask, decision, deadline, or next step explicit.
5. Remove hidden reasoning, internal workflow, and unsupported promises.
6. Check whether a shorter version would work better.

## Review Checks

When reviewing text, flag:

- unclear audience or purpose;
- missing ask or next step;
- unsupported claim;
- excessive background;
- tone mismatch;
- ambiguity that could change meaning;
- sensitive information exposure;
- repeated ideas;
- weak ordering;
- wording that implies approval or commitment too early.
