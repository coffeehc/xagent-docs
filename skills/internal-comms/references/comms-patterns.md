# Internal Comms Patterns

## 3P Update

Use for compact team updates.

- Progress: what moved forward.
- Plans: what happens next.
- Problems: blockers, risks, or support needed.

Keep Problems specific and actionable.

## Announcement

Use for launches, policy changes, org updates, or workflow changes.

Structure:

- headline
- what changed
- why it matters
- who is affected
- when it happens
- action needed
- where to ask questions

## Newsletter

Use for multi-item internal updates.

Structure:

- opening note
- highlights
- key updates
- people or team notes
- upcoming dates
- resources
- ask or feedback channel

Keep each item short. Link or reference details rather than embedding long background.

## FAQ

Use when employees likely have repeated questions.

Structure:

- summary
- question and answer pairs
- policy/source status
- unknowns or pending decisions
- contact point

Answers should be direct. If an answer is not approved, mark it as draft.

## Incident Summary

Use for internal incident or issue communication.

Structure:

- what happened
- current status
- user/customer/team impact
- what changed or was fixed
- what remains unknown
- next update timing
- owner/contact

Avoid unsupported root cause claims. Do not assign blame unless the source material explicitly does and the user asks to include it.

## Leadership Update

Use for high-visibility internal updates from leaders.

Structure:

- message in one sentence
- business/context reason
- impact on teams
- what leaders are asking people to do
- next checkpoint

Use clear, calm language. Avoid corporate filler.
