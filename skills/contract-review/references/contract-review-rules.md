# Contract Review Rules

## Contract Map

Capture:

- parties, affiliates, signatories, notices, and roles;
- document type, status, version, date, effective date, term, renewal, and termination;
- governing law, venue, dispute process, language precedence, and order of precedence when stated;
- referenced documents, exhibits, schedules, policies, data terms, order forms, SOWs, service descriptions, and attachments.

## Clause Families

Review relevant clauses:

- scope, deliverables, acceptance, service levels, support, and change control;
- fees, payment, invoicing, taxes, expenses, late fees, price changes, and renewal pricing;
- confidentiality, publicity, data protection, security, privacy, and breach notice;
- IP ownership, license, feedback, work product, open source, and usage restrictions;
- warranties, disclaimers, limitations of liability, indemnities, insurance, and exclusions;
- term, renewal, suspension, termination for cause, termination for convenience, cure periods, and post-termination duties;
- assignment, subcontracting, audit rights, compliance obligations, sanctions, export controls, anti-bribery, and procurement requirements;
- non-compete, exclusivity, most-favored customer, minimum commitments, and volume obligations when present.

## Issue Spotting

Flag:

- missing definitions or contradictory definitions;
- vague deliverables, acceptance, service levels, support obligations, or owner responsibilities;
- uncapped or asymmetric liability;
- broad indemnity, broad warranty, or unclear exclusions;
- unclear data processing, security, breach notice, or subprocessors;
- IP ownership or license terms that conflict with expected use;
- automatic renewal, price changes, minimum commitments, or termination friction;
- unusual governing law, venue, dispute process, audit rights, or assignment limits;
- missing referenced documents or attachments.

## Risk Notes

Each risk note should state:

- contract location or clause label when available;
- issue;
- why it matters;
- business or operational consequence;
- suggested question or review action;
- review owner such as legal, procurement, privacy, security, finance, tax, HR, or business owner.

Do not state that a clause is legally valid or invalid unless qualified legal review is provided by the user.
