# Meeting Brief Example

## Scenario

The user is meeting with VP Ops to ask for engineering capacity for a Q2 integration.

# Meeting Brief: Q2 Integration Capacity Review

## Private Summary

**Purpose:** secure a decision on Q2 engineering capacity.  
**Primary ask:** ask VP Ops to commit one integration engineer for six weeks.  
**Must achieve:** a named owner and decision date.  
**Key risk:** VP Ops may defer because support backlog is growing.  
**Success signal:** VP Ops agrees to name a capacity owner by Friday.

## Stakeholder Reads

| Stakeholder | Position | Stakes | Likely concerns | Influence | Confidence | Tactical note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| VP Ops | cautious on capacity | support backlog and reliability | timeline and team load | high | inferred: medium | lead with customer-impact evidence and offer phased scope |

## Key Messages

1. The integration is blocked by capacity, not by technical uncertainty.
2. A six-week named owner is enough to unblock the highest-value customer path.
3. If full capacity is not possible, a two-week discovery owner still reduces delivery risk.

## Anticipated Questions And Responses

| Question | Prepared response |
| --- | --- |
| Why now? | Customer commitment and roadmap dependency both hit Q2; waiting pushes integration into the next planning cycle. |

## Confirm Before Use

- VP Ops current support backlog position is inferred from prior recap, not directly confirmed.
