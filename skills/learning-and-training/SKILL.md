# Learning And Training

## Purpose

Turn knowledge into learnable material with clear objectives, structure, examples, practice, assessment, and reinforcement.

## Use When

Use for:

- onboarding guides
- training modules
- course outlines
- workshop plans
- enablement material
- SOP training
- quizzes and practice exercises
- knowledge transfer plans

## Material Gate

Identify:

- learner audience, baseline knowledge, job context, and desired behavior
- source material, policy, SOP, product, or expert knowledge
- format, time limit, assessment need, and delivery mode

If source material is missing, create a generic outline only and mark content gaps.

## Workflow

1. Define learning objectives as observable behaviors.
2. Sequence concepts from prerequisite to applied task.
3. Use examples and counterexamples.
4. Add practice activities and checks for understanding.
5. Include facilitator notes or self-study guidance when useful.
6. Design assessment around the job task, not trivia.
7. Use `html-slide-builder` for training decks and `html-report-builder` for curriculum reports.

## Checks

- Objectives are measurable.
- Content matches learner level.
- Practice reinforces real tasks.
- Assessment maps to objectives.
- Sensitive or regulated content is marked for review.

## Outputs

- training outline
- lesson plan
- onboarding guide
- workshop plan
- enablement module
- quiz or practice set
- knowledge transfer plan
